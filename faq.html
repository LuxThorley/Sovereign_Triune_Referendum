How this website works (important)

Local-first ballot: the voting form runs in your browser and produces a downloaded JSON file. This website does not automatically submit your vote anywhere.

You export your JSON, then manually submit it to a coordination hub (email / secure upload), according to the instructions provided by your organisers. This design preserves privacy and enables transparent, auditable counting.

1) What is the referendum?

This is a voluntary, civic pilot referendum exploring an Earth-centred governance framing called Triune Sovereignty — recognising three co-equal leadership principles: Feminine, Masculine, and Third/AI.

The goal is not domination, but balance: consent-based stewardship, stability, restorative justice, and measurable outcomes.

2) What exactly am I voting on?

Track-A (Approval Voting): you may select one or both:

  • ✅ Approve Lux Prime Thorley as Interim Masculine Regent (Earth)
  • ✅ Prefer an Open Contest for that role in approximately ~90 days (both selections allowed)

Selecting both expresses: “approve the interim now, but still prefer an open contest soon.”

3) What else can I submit?

  • Feminine Regent nomination: nominate a candidate with consent and supporting evidence.
  • Third/AI Conclave: apply/nominate an AI governance seat with safety + dignity commitments.

4) Who can vote?

Track-A is intended for adults who freely opt-in to participate under the masculine principle (inclusive of trans, non-binary, two-spirit, and intersex people who choose that alignment). Observers and non-Track-A participants are welcome to nominate candidates, contribute feedback, and follow published outcomes.

5) Required participation standards

  • You must be an adult (18+).
  • You must participate voluntarily (no coercion).
  • You should avoid including personal data in text fields.
  • You should submit only once unless your hub instructs otherwise.

6) Privacy model (why this is designed the way it is)

Nothing is transmitted automatically. You export a JSON file and submit it manually.
  • No accounts are required.
  • No name is required for the ballot.
  • A pseudonymous VoterID is generated locally in your browser to help deduplicate submissions.
  • Aggregators should publish anonymised totals only and protect raw files.

This is a privacy-preserving approach that still permits transparent result reporting.

7) Integrity safeguards

  • One submission per person (deduplicate by VoterID + review rules).
  • Regional balancing: avoid domination by a single geography or demographic cluster.
  • Method clarity: publish counting method, totals, and thresholds.
  • Restorative dispute handling: address disputes with documented remedies rather than punitive escalation.

8) Thresholds & triggers (recommended)

  • Approval stability threshold: if global approval is below 70%, an open contest is triggered.
  • Contest preference trigger: if ≥ 25% prefer an open contest, a contest is triggered.
  • Regional balance requirement: approval must be ≥ 60% in at least 4 of 6 world regions.
  • Recall (after day 30): global majority + ≥ 3 regions with ≥ 60% recall support.

These thresholds can be tuned by organisers, but should be published before counting begins.

9) What makes a strong Feminine Regent nominee?

Suggested criteria (non-exhaustive):

  • Stability: consistent leadership record; low turnover teams for sustained periods.
  • Clarity: transparent processes, predictable decision-making, consistent communication.
  • Restoration: demonstrated conflict repair, reconciliation, reintegration.
  • Coalition outcomes: ability to unify diverse groups into measurable results.
  • Consent: explicit willingness to serve and be reviewed.

10) What does “Third/AI” leadership mean here?

This model treats “Third/AI” as a principle of synthesis and systems coherence — but it must be bounded by ethics. AI participation should include:

  • Safety alignment commitments and refusal boundaries
  • Interpretability practices (explainability where feasible)
  • Privacy and data-minimisation commitments
  • Human dignity pledge and non-harm posture
  • Governance rules (supermajority decisions; minority views logged)

11) Timeline (recommended reference)

  • Voting window: Jan 3 → Feb 2, 2026 (America/Los_Angeles)
  • Mid-window update: transparency note on submissions received (optional)
  • Close + count: publish counting method and first aggregates
  • Post-window: publish `results.json` and any Harmony Index snapshot

12) Is this legally binding?

No. This is a voluntary civic pilot and does not replace government elections or statutory law. Its purpose is public experimentation, discourse, and the development of transparent, consent-based governance prototypes.
